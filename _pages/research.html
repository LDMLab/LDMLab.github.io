Prevalence-Induced Concept Change

In this study we're interested in exploring how people make judgements about categories. Where do people draw the line between categories like blue and purple, or what's more, between good and bad? How do these categories change as we age and how are they affected by an ever-changing world?

See Sean's submission to CogSci 2020 and CRDH conference poster




Age differences in decision-making strategies

In this study we are interested in studying how young adults and old adults engage in different decision-making strategies. Why do older adults rely more strongly on a habitual decision-making strategy than younger adults do? What is the neural mechanism supporting this shift towards habitual decision-making?

See Alexa's International Symposium on Motivational and Cognitive Control poster.


Cost benefit analysis in cognitive control

Every day life requires that we balance effort and reward. While some situations call for habitual response, others require us to engage in goal-directed behaviour. In this project, we are interested in how individual differences affect how we balance the effort that people put into actions against the potential benefits they reap from performing these actions.
