Effort-Reward Trade-Off

Every day life requires that we balance effort and reward. While some situations call for habitual response, others require us to engage in goal-directed behaviour. Importantly, our ability and willingness to balance these different types of behaviour differs from person to person. In this project, we are interested in how individual differences affect how we balance the effort that people put into actions against the potential benefits they reap from performing these actions.

The Learning of Latent States

Imagine that you are going to your favourite Italian restaurant for dinner. You go there often and always remember the food to be good. Of course, some days the food is better than other and you normally just chalk this up to the chef's mood. But one day, you order your favourite spaghetti dish, you take a bite, and spit it out! It's inedible! Quickly, you start to think that something much more drastic than a change in the chef's mood must have happened.

What you've done here is estimate the latent state (the overall quality of the food) from specific instances. Each meal you ordered helped inform your overall representation of the restaurant's food. But, when a big and unexpected change occured (eating the bad spaghetti), you quickly shifted your representation.

In this project, we are interested if and how different people represent these latent states and how it affects their behaviour.
